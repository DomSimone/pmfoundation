Our Philosophy

Key Functions of Citizen in Championing Sustainable Development Goals

In order for the Citizen of Africa to realise their effective role as the Champions of Sustainable Development in Africa, it is our belief that the following functions must be adopted and advocated for:

1. Advancing meaningful partnerships with Governments and Civil Society for the realization of sustainable development.

Government and Civil Society have an irrevocable role to play as collaborators in the Sustainable Development Agenda. Both sides of the spectrum have access to constituencies which the other may not have the best of ease if they were to attempt the task in solitude. It is therefore important that civil society, with special emphasis on young people and women, be strategically positioned in both Government as well as Civil Society Organizations for the ensuring of wholesome efforts towards development for all. It is essential that both entities refrain from marginalizing the other when it comes to this matter, and therefore the Foundation will be a key instrument in achieving this much needed balance.

2. The strengthening of Active Citizenry & Civil Society leadership development programs for the empowerment of an effective voice that will adequately serve their constituents.

As an old African proverb says, “Until lions tell their own stories, tales of the hunt shall always glorify the hunter.” It is of great importance that ordinary citizens assume ownership of Sustainable Development in their communities, cities, provinces and nations. Without a solid representation of goodwill on the ground, it will be impossible for the structure of well constructed policy frameworks to last. We must ensure that there is an effective mainstreaming of the person on the ground in the consultation process of policy making and adoption, as well as the implementation thereof.

3. Establishment of an efficient succession plan for the assurance of a well established Sustainable Development Framework.

Development cannot be sustainable if it cannot be passed on. It is essential that in our entire decision making, we have the understanding that we are borrowing the earth from our descendants. The power of Citizen is the ability to champion this cause bearing in mind that we are in the process of inheriting the earth from our elders as the focus of development is now sharply turned towards us in our current status. It is therefore with the understanding of the passions, frustrations, hopes and dreams shared among the people of African soil, situated all over the world, that we as leaders champion the improvement of our societies for a better chance at wholesome development for generations to come.

Many battles of development will be overcome in our lifetime. However, the ones that are passed on to the ones who will come after us must not be handed over without providing the necessary empowerment for the championing of development in their day and age. Therefore Sustainable Development will find meaning in our ability to pass on our passion, skills and wisdom onto the next generation.
